### Introduction

As higher education scales, the challenge of providing personalized instruction to a diverse student body has intensified. Adaptive Learning Systems (ALS) promise a solution by dynamically adjusting the pace, difficulty, and content of instruction based on individual learner interactions.

However, the efficacy of ALS, particularly in technical fields like Computer Science where problem-solving is non-linear, remains varied. This systematic review aggregates finding from 45 peer-reviewed studies to identify:
1. Common architectural implementations of ALS.
2. Measurable impacts on student retention and grade distribution.
3. Challenges in adopting ALS in traditional university ecosystems.

### Methodology

We conducted a PRISMA-compliant systematic review, querying databases such as IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, and ERIC for publications between 2015 and 2024. 

### Key Findings

- **Algorithmic Dominance**: Bayesian Knowledge Tracing (BKT) remains the most common underlying algorithm for modeling student knowledge, though deep learning approaches (like DKT) are gaining traction.
- **Improved Retention**: Programs utilizing ALS saw an average 12% improvement in retention rates for introductory programming courses (CS1).
- **The "Cold Start" Problem**: A significant barrier to implementation is the cold start problem—ALS require vast amounts of historical student data to accurately calibrate their difficulty curves initially.
